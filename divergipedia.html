Divergipedia

Divergipedia is a living reference of original terms that name real, repeatable cognitive and behavioral patterns.

These terms exist to compress complexity, increase clarity, and give people usable language for how their brains actually behave.

Entries may be refined, merged, expanded, or retired over time. This is intentional. Human cognition isn’t static, and neither is the language used to describe it.

A

Admin Alley

noun

A deliberately contained window of time reserved for low-dopamine administrative tasks that would otherwise leak into everything else.

Admin Alley exists to keep necessary but cognitively draining work from haunting the rest of the day. Email, forms, billing portals, scheduling. These tasks are done on purpose, in one place, for a fixed amount of time.

It contains administrative load within a bounded window so unresolved tasks don’t spill into higher‑value work.

Behavioral basis:
Time-boxing, decision-fatigue reduction, cognitive load management.

Related terms:
Cognitive Drag · Context Cache · Taskache

Contributor: Divergify

Anchor Task

noun

A single stabilizing task chosen to prevent cognitive drift during periods of overload or fragmentation.

Anchor Tasks reduce the cost of deciding what to do next by providing a default action when attention collapses.

Behavioral basis:
Decision simplification, attentional anchoring.

Related terms:
Task Mountain · Next Action Clarity

Contributor: Divergify

B

Body Budget

noun

An internal accounting of physical and sensory capacity that constrains cognitive performance.

When the body budget is depleted, willpower and focus degrade regardless of motivation.

Behavioral basis:
Physiological load, self-regulation limits.

Related terms:
Brain Battery · Sensory Audit

Contributor: Divergify

C

Chaos Control

noun

The practice of shaping environments and systems to reduce unnecessary cognitive friction rather than relying on self-control.

It reduces reliance on willpower by modifying cues, defaults, and environment so desired actions become the path of least resistance.

Behavioral basis:
Environmental design, friction management.

Related terms:
Setup Macro · Friction Hunt

Contributor: Divergify

Cognitive Drag

noun

The background mental resistance caused by unresolved, unscheduled, or ambiguous obligations.

It accumulates as open loops occupy working memory, slowing decisions and execution even when no active task is in progress.

Behavioral basis:
Working memory saturation, open-loop tension.

Related terms:
Admin Alley · Context Cache

Contributor: Divergify

E

Escape Velocity

noun

The minimum activation energy required to overcome cognitive inertia and enter sustained task engagement.

Small, concrete starter steps lower the initiation threshold and convert inertia into forward motion.

Behavioral basis:
Initiation thresholds, momentum dynamics.

Related terms:
Starter Step · Task Mountain

Contributor: Divergify

G

Gremlin Mode

noun

A dysregulated state marked by impulsivity, irritability, and reduced executive oversight.

Characterized by heightened arousal and narrowed options; typically resolves as regulation returns.

Behavioral basis:
Stress response activation, inhibitory control failure.

Related terms:
Autopilot Mode · Kind Mode

Contributor: Divergify

H

Hyperfocus Tunnel

noun

A narrowed attentional state in which external cues are deprioritized in favor of sustained internal engagement.

Highly productive when bounded; often difficult to exit promptly without explicit cues or limits.

Behavioral basis:
Attentional locking, reward sensitivity.

Related terms:
Context Cache · Transition Buffer

Contributor: Divergify

M

Momentum Thief

noun

Any interruption, decision, or context switch that disproportionately disrupts forward progress.

Even brief interruptions can impose large re-entry costs before focus and flow are restored.

Behavioral basis:
Context switching cost.

Related terms:
Transition Buffer · Admin Alley

Contributor: Divergify

P

Procrastiplanning

noun

Planning behavior that creates the sensation of progress while avoiding task initiation.

Produces the feeling of control while deferring uncertainty and effort required to start.

Behavioral basis:
Avoidance substitution.

Related terms:
Escape Velocity · Task Mountain

Contributor: Divergify

R

Rumination Redirect

noun

A deliberate shift from repetitive thought toward low-load physical or cognitive activity.

Shifts attentional channels to interrupt looping thoughts and reduce arousal.

Behavioral basis:
Attentional reorientation.

Related terms:
Grounding Ritual · Kind Mode

Contributor: Divergify

T

Task Mountain

noun

A task perceived as disproportionately large due to unclear scope or undefined entry points.

Perceived size drops when scope is clear and a concrete entry point exists.

Behavioral basis:
Cognitive load amplification.

Related terms:
Starter Step · Escape Velocity

Contributor: Divergify

Taskache

noun

The low-grade discomfort caused by holding too many tasks in mind without externalizing them.

Internal pressure builds as tasks remain unexternalized; offloading reduces load and frees attention.

Behavioral basis:
Working memory saturation.

Related terms:
Cognitive Drag · Context Cache

Contributor: Divergify

Z

Zero Day Prevention

noun

The practice of ensuring at least one meaningful action occurs daily to maintain continuity.

Preserves continuity and identity with minimal daily action, reducing restart costs after lapses.

Behavioral basis:
Identity reinforcement, streak preservation.

Related terms:
One Thing Rule · Victory Log

Contributor: Divergify

About Divergipedia’s language system

Divergipedia is a curated language system designed to name real, repeatable cognitive and behavioral patterns. It is editorially maintained and not crowdsourced.

Terms may be submitted for review. Submissions are voluntary and unpaid. Accepted terms are credited and may be edited for clarity or consistency.

Divergipedia draws from behavioral science, cognitive psychology, neuroscience, and systems theory. It does not claim to invent these mechanisms. It translates them into usable language.
